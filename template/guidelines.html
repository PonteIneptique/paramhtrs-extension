Text Normalization Guidelines

Some examples will be provided in pseudo-XML to clarify the alternative between original text (<orig/>) and corrected text (<reg/>).

1. Space and Character Normalization

Normalize spaces between words and remove doubled consonants in specific language practices like Italian.

<choice><orig>arriva</orig><reg>a riva</reg></choice>

2. Normalization of Letters

Convert u/v and i/j according to modern spelling conventions.

3. Abbreviation Resolution

Expand abbreviations where possible. If uncertain (because of a lack of context, specifically for names), retain a modern abbreviation.

<choice><orig>.S. .B.</orig><reg>Sanctus B.</reg></choice>

4. Hyphen and Line Break Handling

Remove hyphens at the end of lines and click merge on the next line.

5. Accent Normalization

Introduce accents where applicable to the language.

6. Apostrophe Addition

For languages that use apostrophes, restore them where necessary.

<choice><orig>larive</orig><reg>l'arivé</reg></choice>

7. Missing Letter Restoration

Add inferred missing letters, such as initials, if they can be easily deduced.

8. Punctuation Spacing

Ensure the normalisation of spacing around punctuation marks: remove any space before them and restore a space after if necessary.

9. Regional Variations

Consider regional variations when resolving abbreviations or spelling inconsistencies.

<choice><orig>q̃e</orig><reg>que</reg></choice>

10. HTR Inference Correction

Correct the text only if it is easily correctable and improves readability.
